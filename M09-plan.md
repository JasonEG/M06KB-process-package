# M09 Execution Plan — SOPs in the AI Era

**Module:** 09 — SOPs in the AI Era
**Deliverable:** A09 — Video Essay (5 minutes)
**Due:** Sunday, March 15, 2026 by 11:59 PM ET
**Graded artifact:** Video only — no outline, speaker notes, or prompt log required for submission

---

## Assignment Summary

Produce one 5-minute video essay anchored in a recent source (published September 2025 or later). The essay must:

1. Introduce the source and summarize its core point
2. Connect it to SOPs and operational process work
3. Take a clear position of your own
4. Close with a practical takeaway for managers, teams, or process owners
5. Cite the source visibly in the video (opening or closing slide)

**Not a summary exercise.** The argument matters more than production value.

---

## Execution Steps

### Step 1 — Source Selection (20–30 min)
- Skim 3–5 recent pieces (Sept 2025 or later) on AI in knowledge work, automation, governance, documentation, or operational change
- Choose the one that gives the clearest argument to build from — ideally one with tension to respond to, qualify, or push back on
- Strong topic areas: AI agents + SOPs, RAG + knowledge management, AI governance, human oversight, onboarding with AI

**Practical test:** If the source can be summarized in one sentence without losing much, it is too thin.

### Step 2 — Claim Building (20 min)
Draft:
- **Thesis** (one sentence): What is your core argument?
- **Three supporting points**: Evidence from the source + your own reasoning
- **One practical takeaway**: What should a manager or process owner actually do differently?

### Step 3 — Outline the Essay Structure
Standard 5-minute structure (~750 words at normal speaking pace):

| Segment | Time | Content |
|---------|------|---------|
| Opening + source intro | ~45 sec | Hook, introduce source, state thesis |
| Point 1 | ~60 sec | What the source says; what it gets right |
| Point 2 | ~60 sec | What it misses or where you push back |
| Point 3 | ~60 sec | What it suggests about the future of SOPs |
| Practical takeaway + close | ~45 sec | Actionable conclusion; restate thesis |

### Step 4 — Recording (rehearsal + final)
- Practice opening and closing before recording
- Format options: talking-head, narrated slides, screen recording with voiceover, or combination
- One clean re-record if structure or audio needs fixing; don't over-polish

### Step 5 — Submission
- Upload to Canvas or submit an accessible link (no permission prompt for instructor)
- Confirm source citation is visible in the video itself
